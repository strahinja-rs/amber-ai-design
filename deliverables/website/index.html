After Initial Brainstorming with Claude

The starting point

Amber is fossilized tree resin — nature's way of preserving moments in time. Warm to the touch. Golden when light passes through.
  • Form: Pendant, jewelry aesthetic, warm colors
  • Positioning: "Wear it when it matters"
  • Initial tagline: "Preserve moments"

01-PHILOSOPHY.md

The core insight

The privacy problem with AI wearables isn't the device — it's the framing. Humane Pin, Friend, Rabbit R1 created a "third presence" in conversations. Creepy.

Philosophy Decision

Device is part of you, not separate. Like glasses help you see → Amber helps you remember.

02-JTBD.md

Jobs to Be Done

Framework: "When I..., I want..., so I can..."

When I'm in a conversation that matters, I want to preserve exactly what was said, so I can be fully present and recall it perfectly later.

Emotional Job

Peace of mind, not anxiety. I won't forget.

Research → Pivot

What the data told us

Market Landscape

  • Plaud Note: $10M+ sales (audio-only)
  • Meta acquired Limitless, killed hardware
  • Companion positioning = saturated

The Insight

  • You can't aim a pendant like a camera
  • Audio captures words, tone, pauses
  • 40-80% medical info forgotten immediately

Pivot Decision

Audio-first. No camera. The value is in the words.

05-BRAND.md

Brand personality

  • Warm — Not cold tech. A comfort object.
  • Quiet — No personality, no voice, no opinions.
  • Honest — Doesn't disguise what it is.
  • Secure — Wearing it means peace of mind.
  • Timeless — Like amber itself.

Brand Voice

Say vs Don't Say

Say

  • Preserve, Keep, Remember
  • "Wear it when it matters"
  • "Peace of mind"
  • "Be present"

Don't Say

  • Record, Capture, Store

  • "Always with you" (surveillance)

06-PRD.md

Summary

Key decisions

  • No camera ← Cameras trigger surveillance response
